# WHY

i was asked, several times, "why."

i was asked it most recently by a man at a gas station outside fresno who saw my laptop screen through the passenger window. he was not asking about volume. he wanted to know why anyone would do this. he had bought a sandwich. it was 11:42pm. the sandwich tax, by my reckoning, was zero, because nobody is taxing sandwiches yet. that's coming. that's all coming.

i told him i was making the world's most overengineered volume controller and he nodded the way you nod at someone who has just told you they are starting a podcast about their dreams. then he said "good luck with that" and walked back to his truck. i sat in my car and added the medieval lever.

---

## why a knob

a slider is a line. a knob is a *commitment.* you must rotate. you must engage with rotation. rotation has *consequences* in a way that translation does not. you cannot rotate something accidentally. (you can. but the knob will know.)

i tried using a slider for forty minutes once. a single linear gesture, end to end, value 0 to value 100. it was *fine.* and that is the problem. fine is the enemy. fine got us into this mess. fine is why apartment buildings have one elevator. fine is why the greeks stopped building amphitheaters. nobody ever erected a monument to fine.

i replaced the slider with a knob and within the hour i had three faces drawn for it and a name for the company and the name was VOLUMETRIC HUBRIS and the slogan was "we apologize to your roommates."

---

## why eight realities

eight is not the right number. nine is the right number. but i ran out of metaphors at seven and added the forbidden button to make it eight because eight has a hum to it that nine does not. the forbidden button doesn't really do anything. the forbidden button is the *theology* of the project. the forbidden button is what you press when you are tired of choosing.

i was going to add a ninth scene called "the desert" where you walk a small camel and the camel's mood is the volume but i did not have time. the camel is in another build. the camel is fine. the camel is being cared for. please do not ask about the camel.

---

## why the knob has feelings

so it could betray you.

next question.

---

## why the procedural pad

at 3am on a tuesday i looked at a folder of `.wav` files i had bought on a license-free site and i felt — i don't know how to describe this — *contempt.* not for the files. for me. for who i would be if i shipped this with sample files. there is something profoundly unserious about a piece of work this serious using royalty-free pads from a vendor named like a discontinued energy drink.

so i wrote five oscillators in the audio engine. they breathe. you cannot tell, but they breathe. there is a low-frequency oscillator on the filter cutoff that completes a cycle every fourteen seconds, which is approximately the resting respiration rate of a sleeping mid-size dog. i told no one this. it is in the code now and so it is in this document now and so this is technically the first time i am admitting to it. the dog is fine. the dog is in another build.

---

## why 9001 percent

because there is no other number that is funny in exactly the way i needed a number to be funny in 2026.

---

## why the volume tax

every interface should make you suspect, at all times, that something is being charged to your card. the exact amount is irrelevant. the *presence* of a counter is the point. the counter says: this is real. this is being measured. someone, somewhere, is watching.

it is also, technically, $0.0001 per adjustment. if you spent the next eleven days continuously adjusting the volume by 1% per second you would owe roughly $9.50, which is a cup of coffee in any city you would actually want to live in. i ran the math. i then deleted the math because it was making the project feel like a startup pitch and the entire point is that this is not a startup pitch. this is a *temple*.

---

## why mute is a boss fight

because muting a thing should require that you have *earned the silence.* every thirteen-year-old with a phone can mute a tiktok. that is not silence. that is dismissal. silence is what you negotiate with the void after a long argument that you have, frankly, both lost.

the boss is called the GUARDIAN OF SOUND. it has three phases. its third-phase mechanic is, currently, in the boss-battle README which is itself in a spreadsheet on a different computer that i locked myself out of three weeks ago. but i remember the gist. i remember the *vibe*. you will defeat the GUARDIAN OF SOUND. you will earn your silence. you will weep, briefly, and then you will turn the volume back up because silence is a season, not a destination.

---

## why the forbidden button

i needed to know how many people would press it.

---

## why now

because the world is, by most measures, slightly louder than it should be, and slightly less interesting than it should be, and the small acts of taking something we have all agreed is uninteresting (a slider) and treating it like it matters (a *knob*) are the only acts left.

the man at the gas station finished his sandwich. i added the cosmic orbit. somewhere a knob is being preened over. somewhere a peasant is yelling BALANCE ACHIEVED. it is, by my computer, 2026. it is, by everyone else's calendar, 2026 also. there is no excuse for the sliders we are still shipping. i refuse to participate.

---

i am told the sandwich was a turkey club.
